A Story about help between countries...

Start the story

What you will be told about...

If you check today's news, you will probably see things like "Trump tweeted something stupid again" or "a war as been declared". Generally, there are not a lof of good news in the actuality. So we thought it'd be nice, for once, to talk, not about countires making war, but countries helping each other. Help is to be understand in a very large sense. It could be an economic help, it could be help during a war and so on...


To make our work, we uses a database that count a lot of articles from 1979 to 2016. They talk about anything, but we selected articles that talked about a country helping another one. We downloaded a little part of this database (from 2 TB we ended up with 500 MB). We parsed the data we needed with scripts. They are all avaliable on our repository. If you wish to fork/clone our repo, you can go here. Have a coffee while downloading, it may take a while :)

Enjoy our story !

Who talks the more about help ?

Let's see first who's the more optimistic. There were thoudands and thousands of newspapers and websites downloaded. At first, we wanted to put them all but it made the site crash (obvisously). So we decided to get only the most represented. Here are the 30 newspapers which have been the more cited. Most of them, as we can see, are english-speaking. Most of them come from England or USA. It's also interesting to note that the first provider (and from far) is a website. A little surprise on this chart : the third newspaper is an African website. We were surprised.

For the following chart and with what we see, we expect to see a lot of english-speaking countries heping the world.

All these infos have been taken from January 2013 to December 2016. Before that, the database did not write down the sources.

That's nice, but, concretely...

Yeah, we are coming to the main point. In the chart below, you can select a country and see which other countries it helped or by which other countries it was helped. To perform this magic trick, you can simply press the Toogle button the chart. Note that for some countries, it seems that they did not help anyone or did not get any help. That's not because they are selfish, but, as wee see previously, the data come generally from USA or England, so little countries could have been forgotten. Moreover, some countries were grouped as continent, so now they do not appear here. That's a thing you need to remember; in this chart, country is represent individually and not as an union.

But now you can have a global idea of how much love there is in this world.

Amazing ! Can we be more specified ?

Of course we can !

Here, we have the the top 25 of aid between countries. Why only 25 ? Same reason as before, with more data, impossible to load the page. Anyway, that was expected : USA is a really helpfull country ! Sepcial mention to our country, which was really helped by Canada over the last years...



In this chart, we let the continent and the unions of countries. So we can see that the continent of Africa is often helped, but not a specific country.

Note that this chart is not exactly correct : we omitted the first couple, because it was over represented and it made our graph looks really bad. Can you guess which countries it is ? Of course, it's something like USA helps someone. Yes but who's that someone ? You give up ? With 499'261 articles, the winner is USA - Uzbekistan. You did not expect that, did you ? Well, us neither. But since the fall of the communism and until the middle of the 2000s, these two were having good relations.

You keep talking about aid, which is nice, but what kind of aid is it ?

That's a good question. Since a lot of data did not have source, most of the aid provided is not specified. But here, you can see a little chart with what we could get. Basically, there are two big categories : economic and military. We were expecting that. To go further, it could be interesting to see, by country, what can of aid it provides and to whom. But that will be for an other story.
